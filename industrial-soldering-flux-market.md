<p><p>The industrial soldering flux market encompasses various types, including paste, liquid, powder, and others. Paste flux is commonly used for its adhesive properties, providing excellent coverage and solderability in electronic assemblies. Liquid flux is employed for its ease of application, often in wave soldering processes, ensuring even distribution. Powder flux is typically used in specialized applications, offering high melting points for robust joints. Other forms may include gel and rosin flux, each catering to specific industrial requirements and processes.</p></p>

<p><p>The industrial soldering flux market serves various applications including fusion welding, pressure welding, brazing, and others. Fusion welding utilizes flux to prevent oxidation and enhance metal bonding during the melting of base materials. Pressure welding employs flux in areas where metallic surfaces are joined under pressure, promoting strong adhesion. Brazing involves the use of flux to facilitate the joining of dissimilar metals at elevated temperatures.

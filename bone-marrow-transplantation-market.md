<p><p>The bone marrow transplantation market comprises two main types: allogeneic and autologous transplants. Allogeneic bone marrow transplantation involves transferring stem cells from a compatible donor, often used to treat conditions like leukemia and lymphoma. In contrast, autologous bone marrow transplantation utilizes the patient's own stem cells, typically harvested before intensive treatment, to minimize the risk of rejection. Both methods serve critical roles in treating hematologic diseases, with market dynamics influenced by factors like technology advancements and patient demand.</p></p>

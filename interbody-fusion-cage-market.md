<p><p>An Interbody Fusion Cage is a medical device used in spinal surgeries, specifically in procedures aimed at fusing adjacent vertebrae. These cages are typically made of materials such as titanium or PEEK (polyether ether ketone) and are designed to provide structural support while promoting bone growth between vertebrae. They are inserted into the intervertebral space and help restore the natural height of the disc while stabilizing the spine.</p><p>The Interbody Fusion Cage Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 3.60% during the forecast period. <p><p>The interbody fusion cage market comprises four primary types: lumbar, cervical, thoracolumbar, and thoracic. Lumbar cages are designed for the lower back, addressing conditions like herniated discs. Cervical cages target the neck region, often used in procedures for disc degeneration. Thoracolumbar cages cater to the thoracolumbar region, combining features of both lumbar and thoracic cages. Thoracic cages focus on the upper and middle back, stabilizing the spine in those areas. Each type caters to specific anatomical needs and surgical applications.</p></p>

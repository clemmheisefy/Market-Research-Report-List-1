<p><p>The Lightweight Synthetic Acrylic Fibers market primarily utilizes two spinning methods: wet spinning and dry spinning. Wet spinning involves dissolving polymer in a solvent and extruding it into a coagulating bath, producing fibers with excellent uniformity and strength. In contrast, dry spinning entails dissolving the polymer in a solvent that evaporates upon extrusion, resulting in lightweight fibers suitable for various applications. Both methods cater to distinct market needs, influencing the production processes and end-use characteristics of acrylic fibers.</p></p>

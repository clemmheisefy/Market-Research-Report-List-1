<p><p>Cord blood banking services encompass three main market types: private banks, public banks, and hybrid banks. Private banks collect and store cord blood for individual families, offering personalized access for potential future medical needs. Public banks, on the other hand, accept donations which are stored for use by anyone in need, promoting broader community benefits. Hybrid banks combine elements of both models, allowing families to both store their cord blood privately and contribute to a public registry, enhancing availability for diverse patient needs.</p></p>

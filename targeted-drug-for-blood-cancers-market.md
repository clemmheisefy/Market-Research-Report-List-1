<p><p>The targeted drug market for blood cancers primarily includes two key types: monoclonal antibodies and small-molecule drugs. Monoclonal antibodies are lab-produced molecules that can specifically bind to cancer cells, marking them for destruction by the immune system. Conversely, small-molecule drugs are chemically synthesized compounds designed to interfere with specific pathways involved in cancer cell growth and survival. Together, these therapies provide more precise treatment options, enhancing efficacy while minimizing damage to healthy tissues in patients with blood cancers.</p></p>
